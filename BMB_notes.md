## Hamiltonian dynamics

A lot of the stuff on the [wikipedia page](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamiltonian_mechanics) is irrelevant/too advanced, but you might look at the sections on "Basic physical interpretation" and "Hamiltonian as the total system energy"; in particular, I'm interested in whether we can get sensible intuitive definitions for energy, potential function, etc. for the SIR model and other ecological systems.

  You might start by seeing if you can establish the conditions for energy conservation etc. using (1) a simple physical system for which you know it should work (2D pendulum/simple oscillator, 3D pendulum, etc.); (2) the Hamiltonian for the Lotka-Volterra predator prey system (see Baigent [2010] ref below, read the first section); (3) **eventually** the SIR system.

## SIR equations

For the SIR equations, there are also many, many, many places to look. You could try Earn 2008 or Bjørnstad 2018 (available online via SpringerLink) or Rohani and Keeling's textbook or ...

Earn, David J. D. “A Light Introduction to Modelling Recurrent Epidemics.” In Mathematical Epidemiology, edited by J. M. Morel, F. Takens, and B. Teissier, vol. 1945, edited by Fred Brauer, Pauline Van Den Driessche, and Jianhong Wu. Lecture Notes in Mathematics. Springer Berlin Heidelberg, 2008. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-540-78911-6_1.

Bjørnstad, Ottar N. Epidemics: Models and Data Using R. 1st ed. 2018 edition. Springer, 2018.

Copilot also recommended Edelstein-Keshet, Leah. Mathematical Models in Biology. Classics in Applied Mathematics. Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics, 2005. https://doi.org/10.1137/1.9780898719147. (I have a copy if the library doesn't)
